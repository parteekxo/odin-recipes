Kadhi Pakora

Description

Discover the comforting and tangy flavors of Kadhi Pakora, a classic North Indian dish made from gram flour (besan) and yogurt-based curry, combined with crispy pakoras (fritters). This recipe takes you through the process of creating light, golden-brown pakoras that are soaked in a smooth, spiced yogurt gravy, resulting in a perfect balance of flavors and textures.

Whether served with steamed rice or roti, Kadhi Pakora is a heartwarming meal that’s both satisfying and packed with authentic Indian spices. With this easy-to-follow recipe, you’ll be able to recreate this traditional favorite at home. Ideal for a family dinner or a cozy meal on a rainy day, Kadhi Pakora is sure to bring comfort to your table. Let’s dive in and make this delicious dish!

Ingredients

  • 1 cup besan (gram flour) (for pakoras and kadhi)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped, for pakoras)
  • 1.5 cups yogurt (preferably sour, for kadhi)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds (for pakoras and tempering)
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder (for pakoras and kadhi)
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder (for pakoras and kadhi)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder (for pakoras and kadhi)
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda (optional, for pakoras)
  • Salt to taste (for pakoras and kadhi)
  • Water to make the pakora batter and to thin the kadhi
  • Oil (for frying pakoras and for tempering)
  • 4 tablespoons besan (for kadhi)
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee (for tempering)
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds (for tempering)
  • 1 dried chili (for tempering)
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds (methi seeds) (for tempering)
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing) (for tempering)
  • 8-10 curry leaves (for tempering)
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala (option, for tempering)
  • 2-3 garlic cloves (chopped, optional, for tempering)
  • 1-inch piece ginger (finely choped, for tempering)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Pakora Batter:
    • In a mixing bowl, add 1 cup besan.
    • Add finely chopped onion, 1 teaspoon cumin seeds, 1 teaspoon red chili powder, 1 teaspoon corainder powder, 1/2 turmeric powder, 1/4 teaspoon baking soda (if using), and salt to taste.
    • Gradually add water and mix until you get a thick, lump-free batter.
  2. Fry the Pokars:
    • Heat enough oil in a pan for deep frying.
    • Drop spoonfuls of the pakoras into the hot oil. Fry the pakoras until they are goldern brown and crispy.
    • Remove the pakoras with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels.
  3. Prepare the Kadhi:
    • In a bowl, whisk 1.5 cup yogurt until smooth.
    • Add 4 tablespoons besan to the yogurt and mix well.
    • In a large pot, heat 2 tablespoons oil or ghee over medium heat.
    • Add 1 teaspoon cumin seeds, 1 dried chili, 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeda, and a pinch of asafoetida (hing). Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
    • Slowly add the yogurt-besan mixture to the pot, stiring continuously to avoid lumps.
    • Add 1 teaspoon red chili powder, 1 teaspoon coriander powder, 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder, and salt to taste.
    • Bring the kadhi to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally. Add water as needed to adjust the consistency.
  4. Add Pakoras to Kadhi:
    • Gently add the fried pakoras to the simmering kadhi.
    • Let them cook in the kadhi for about 5-10 minutes so they absorb some of the flavors.
  5. Temper the Kadhi:
    • In a small pan, heat 2 tablespoons oil or ghee.
    • Add 1 teaspoon mustard seeds, 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds, and 8-10 curry leaves. Sauté until the mustard seeds start to pop.
    • Add 1/2 teaspoon garam masala (optional), 2-3 chopped garlic cloves (optional), and 1-inch piece of finely chopped ginger. Sauté for a minute.
  6. Final Touch:
    • Pour the tempering over the kadhi.
    • Stir gently and let it simmer for another 2-3 minutea.
  7. Serve:
    • Serve the Kadhi Pakora hot with steamed rice or roti
