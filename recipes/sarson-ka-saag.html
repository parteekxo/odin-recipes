Sarson ka Saag

Description

Sarson ka Saag is a beloved Punjabi dish renowned for its rich and hearty flavor. This traditional recipe highlights mustard greens, also known as sarson, which are simmered with a blend of aromatic spices and vegetables to create a comforting and nutritious dish. Often enjoyed with Makki di Roti (cornmeal flatbread), Sarson ka Saag is a quintessential part of Punjabi cuisine that captures the essence of rustic, home-cooked meals.

In this recipe, fresh mustard greens are combined with spinach and optional bathua to enhance the depth of flavor and texture. The saag is seasoned with a mix of spices including cumin, turmeric, and garam masala, and thickened with cornmeal to achieve a satisfying consistency. Finished with a dollop of butter for added richness, this dish is perfect for a cozy meal.

Whether you're familiar with Punjabi cuisine or new to it, Sarson ka Saag is a delightful way to experience traditional Indian flavors. Follow this recipe to bring the warmth and authenticity of Punjabi cooking to your table.

Ingredients

  • Mustard Greens (Sarson) - 500 grams
  • Spinach (Palak) - 250 grams (optional, for a smoother texture)
  • Bathua (Chenopodium) - 100 grams (optional, for added flavor)
  • Onions - 1 large, finely chopped
  • Tomatoes - 2 medium, chopped
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste - 1 tablespoon
  • Green Chili - 2-3, chopped (adjust to taste)
  • Cornmeal (Makki Ka Atta) - 2-3 tablespoons (helps thicken the saag)
  • Ghee or Oil - 2-3 tablespoons
  • Cumin Seeds - 1 teaspoon
  • Turmeric Powder - 1/2 teaspoon
  • Red Chili Powder - 1 teaspoon (adjust to taste)
  • Garam Masala - 1 teaspoon
  • Salt - to taste
  • Fresh Corainder Leaves - for garnish (optional)
  • Butter - for a richer taste (optional)
  • Lemon Juice - for added tanginess (optional)

Steps

  1. Prepare the Greens:
    • Wash the mustard greens, spinach, and bathua thoroughly under running water.
    • Chop the greens coarsely.
  2. Cook the Greens:
    • In a large pot, bring water to a boil.
    • Add the chopped greens to the boiling water and cook for about 10-15 minutes, or until they are tender.
    • Drain the water and let the greens cool slightly.
    • Once cool, blend the greens into a coarse paste using a blender or food processor.
  3. Prepare the Masala:
    • Heat ghee or oil in a large pan over medium heat.
    • Add cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
    • Add finely chopped onions and saut√© until they turn golden brown.
    • Stir in the ginger-garlic paste and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
    • Add the chopped tomatoes and cook until they are soft and the oil starts to separate from the masala.
  4. Add Spices:
    • Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and salt to the masala. Mix well and cook for a minute.
  5. Combine Greens and Masala:
    • Add the blended greens paste to the pan with the masala.
    • Stir well to combine and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  6. Thicken the Saag:
    • Gradually add cornmeal (makki ka atta) to the saag while stirring to avoid lumps.
    • Cook for an additional 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the saag thickens and the cornmeal is well incorporated.
  7. Finish with Garam Masala:
    • Add garam masala and stir well.
    • Adjust salt and spice levels to taste.
    • Cook for another 2-3 minutes.
  8. Garnish and Serve:
    • Garnish with Fresh Coriander Leaves if desired.
    • Serve hot with Makki di Roti (cornmeal flatbread) or steamed rice.
